# Prompts for a Proof-Driven Application

Use these to draft, then always rewrite in your own voice with real specifics.

## 1. ATS keyword extractor
```
Here is a job description:
---
[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]
---
Extract:
1. Hard skills and tools (exact terms).
2. Certifications or qualifications named.
3. Recurring responsibilities.
4. The 8 keywords most likely used by an ATS filter.
Return as a checklist I can compare against my résumé.
```

## 2. Bullet rewriter (claim -> quantified outcome)
```
Rewrite this résumé bullet to lead with a measurable outcome and use an
active verb. Keep it truthful; do NOT invent numbers. If a metric is
missing, ask me one question to surface it instead of guessing.
Bullet: "[PASTE BULLET]"
```

## 3. Proof-mapper
```
Here are my top résumé claims:
---
[PASTE 5 BULLETS]
---
For each claim, suggest ONE type of verifiable proof I could attach or link
(work sample, portfolio piece, metric screenshot, referral). Flag any claim
that has no plausible proof — those are the risky ones.
```

## 4. Follow-up interrogator (survives-the-follow-up test)
```
Act as a skeptical hiring manager. Here is my STAR answer:
---
[PASTE ANSWER]
---
Ask me 4 progressively harder follow-up questions probing: my specific
contribution vs. the team's, trade-offs I made, what went wrong, and what I'd
do differently. Do not accept vague answers.
```

## 5. Voice de-robotizer
```
Rewrite the text below to sound like a specific human professional: concrete
nouns, no filler adjectives, no phrases like 'leverage synergies' or
'passionate about excellence'. Preserve all facts. Keep it under [N] words.
Text: "[PASTE DRAFT]"
```
