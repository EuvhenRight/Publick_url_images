# Junior Developer Judgment-Building Checklist

A lightweight routine for building the "judgment + domain context" that the market increasingly pays for, especially when AI handles first-draft code.

## Daily
- [ ] For at least one AI-generated snippet, write 2 sentences on *why* it works and one way it could fail.
- [ ] Read one code review comment carefully and note the underlying principle.

## Weekly
- [ ] Own one change end-to-end: ticket → PR → merge → verify in production/staging.
- [ ] Review at least one teammate's PR and leave a substantive comment (not just "LGTM").
- [ ] Read one production incident / post-mortem (yours or public, e.g. from engineering blogs).

## Monthly
- [ ] Write a short internal note explaining a system in your domain to a newcomer.
- [ ] Identify one recurring failure mode in your area and propose a guardrail (test, alert, lint rule).
- [ ] Reflect: what decision did I make this month that AI could not have made for me?

## Quarterly
- [ ] Deepen one domain area (business rules, regulatory constraints, user behavior).
- [ ] Update a personal "judgment log" of decisions and their outcomes—your compounding asset.

## The single test
Ask: *If an AI produced this, could I confidently defend, reject, or improve it?* If yes, you are building the experience the market rewards.
