# Sponsorship vs. Ambush: Measuring What You Actually Bought

The LinkedIn post raises a sharp marketing question hiding behind the World Cup story: when you spend big on official sponsorship, are you paying for *share of voice* or for *sales*? This guide turns that provocation into a practical framework you can use to evaluate any large brand-visibility spend.

## The Core Tension

- **Official sponsorship** buys *permission* and *association* — the right to say "official X of Y." It is protected, legal, and often expensive (nine figures at World Cup scale).
- **Ambush / earned tactics** (like Levi's covering its own logo per FIFA clean-site rules, then turning that erasure into content) buy *attention* without the rights fee.

The uncomfortable truth in the post: association does not automatically convert to memory or sales. "Powerade is the official sports drink. Not Gatorade" — and almost nobody remembers, because Gatorade owns the mental category.

## A Decision Framework

Before committing to a sponsorship, score it on four dimensions:

| Dimension | Question | Weight it high if... |
|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| Category ownership | Do we already own the mental category? | We're the leader defending, not the challenger buying legitimacy |
| Activation budget | Do we have 2-3x the rights fee to activate? | Rights alone rarely move sales |
| Measurability | Can we tie exposure to a sales/consideration lift? | We have attribution infrastructure |
| Ambush exposure | Can competitors legally out-shout us? | Clean-site rules create earned-media loopholes |

Rule of thumb: **the rights fee is the entry ticket, not the show.** If you cannot fund activation and measurement, you are buying share of voice you cannot cash.

## Share of Voice vs. Sales: Reconciling the Scoreboards

1. **Define the primary KPI first.** Awareness lift, aided/unaided recall, consideration, or direct sales/conversions. Pick one to optimize.
2. **Instrument a control.** Use holdout geographies or matched-market testing so you can isolate the sponsorship effect.
3. **Track the ambush too.** Measure competitor earned media in the same window. Their spike is your leakage.
4. **Report net, not gross.** Impressions are gross. Incremental consideration and incremental sales are net.

## Takeaways

- Sponsorship is defensive infrastructure for category leaders, and an expensive bet for challengers.
- Earned/ambush creativity can outrank paid presence — plan for it, or exploit it.
- If you can't measure incremental lift, you're buying a story, not a scoreboard.
